Hero Section: Scroll-Driven Timeline

The hero becomes a choreographed sequence as users scroll. Each phase triggers at a scroll threshold.

Timeline Phases

Phase 1 — On Load (0-0.5s) viewport entry

Character-by-Character Headline Reveal

Replace current word-stagger with per-character animation. Each character fades up from 20px below with 0.02s stagger. The strike-through SVG on "HETEK" draws after the word completes. Framer Expert badge scales in with a spring bounce.

Phase 2 — On Load (0.5-1.2s) after headline

Supporting Elements Cascade

Subtitle text fades in line-by-line (mask reveal — overflow:hidden wrapper, inner span translateY). CTA buttons slide up with spring physics, staggered 0.1s apart. Decorative flourishes float in from their respective corners with rotation.

Phase 3 — On Load (1.0-1.8s) overlaps phase 2

Stage / Mockup Entrance

The design/live mode stage scales up from 0.85 with opacity, landing with a subtle bounce. The chrome bar, panels, and canvas elements stagger in from their edges (layers from left, properties from right, canvas fades in center). First auto-toggle fires after the entrance completes.

Phase 4 — On Scroll scroll-linked

Parallax Depth Separation

As user scrolls past hero: headline moves at 0.4x speed (recedes), stage moves at 0.7x (midground), flourishes move at 1.3x (foreground rush). Creates a layered depth-of-field effect. Background gradient shifts hue slightly with scroll progress. The whole hero compresses and fades as it exits viewport.

Design/Live Mode Toggle Enhancement

The existing auto-toggle gets upgraded: instead of an instant swap, the transition uses a 3D card flip (rotateY 180°) or a morph wipe (clipPath circle expanding from the toggle button position). The toggle buttons get a sliding pill indicator (layout animation) showing which mode is active.

Mobile Adaptation

Character-by-character becomes word-by-word (fewer elements to animate). Parallax depth reduced to 2 layers max. Stage entrance simplified to a single scale+fade. All spring physics use stiffer values for snappier feel on lower-powered devices.
